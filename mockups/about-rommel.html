Your dream system—the one built entirely around how you work—is finally possible. It's your process, made smarter.

2001 The First Automation: UUnet Email Workflow

When I started at UUnet in 2001, I was hired to do a specific job in the web server installation and colocation department. I did that job, but in parallel, I also automated the repetitive things that me and my colleagues had to do in that department.

The first sore point I identified were repetitive emails that had to be sent to customers. These emails could be automated into a workflow where data is collected from a database and input into a welcome email that introduced customers to their newly provisioned product, explained how to contact the support staff, and provided guidance on how to use their service.

What This First Automation Did

  • Connected to the customer database to pull provisioning information
  • Automatically generated personalized welcome emails with customer details
  • Included instructions on how to contact support staff
  • Provided guidance on how to use the newly provisioned product
  • Eliminated repetitive manual email composition for the entire team

This wasn't just about saving time—it was about freeing the team to focus on higher-value work. Instead of spending hours crafting similar emails, we could handle more customers and provide better service. This pattern of identifying repetitive work and automating it became the foundation of how I approach every system I build.
